The Restricted Three-Body Problem Part 3

發表於 2022-10

Lagrangian Equilibrium Points

Looking for the equilibrium points in the rotating coordinate frame, so that its position with respect to the two masses keep from a constant distance.

The equation of the motion in the rotating frame are these equations by moving the centrifugal term (\(n^2x\) and \(n^2y\) ) to the right-hand side of equation.

\(x\)-direction:

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq38.1} (\ddot x  -2n\dot y ) = \frac{\partial{V}}{\partial{x}}= - [ \mu_1 \frac{(\mu_2+x)}{r_1^{3}} + \mu_2 \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2^{3}} ] + n^2x \end{equation}\]

\(y\)-direction:

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq38.2}  (\ddot y + 2n \dot x )= \frac{\partial{V}}{\partial{y}}= (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} )\cdot y + n^2y \end{equation} \]

\(z\)-direction:

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq38.3} \ddot z = \frac{\partial{V}}{\partial{z}}= - (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} +  \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} )\cdot  \end{equation} \]

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq39} V(r) = V(x, y, z) \\ = \frac{n^2}{2}(x^2+y^2) + \frac{\mu_1}{r_1}+\frac{\mu_2}{r_2} \end{equation} \]

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq40.1} r_1^2 = (x_1-x)^2 + y^2 + z^2 \\ = (\mu_2+x)^2 + y^2 + z^2 \end{equation} \]

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq40.2} r_2^2 = (x_1-x)^2 + y^2 + z^2 \\ = (x-\mu_1)^2 + y^2 + z^2 \end{equation}\]

where
\(\mu_1=Gm_1=\frac{m_1}{m_1+m_2}\)

\(\mu_2=\bar{\mu}=Gm_1=\frac{m_2}{m_1+m_2}\)

\(\mu_1 + \mu_2 = 1\)

Here, mean motion n is taken as unity \((n=1)\)

The velocity of equilibrium points would be

\[\begin{equation} \dot x = \dot y =\dot z = \ddot x = \ddot y =\ddot z = 0 \end{equation}\]

From Equation , we must have \(z=0\) for the equilibrium points, thus, the equilibrium points are in the orbital plane of two masses. Therefore, based on Equation , Equation and Equation becomes

\[\begin{equation}  \frac{\partial{V}}{\partial{x}} = 0 = -[ \mu_1 \frac{(\mu_2+x)}{r_1^{3}} + \mu_2 \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2^{3}} ] + x \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation}  \frac{\partial{V}}{\partial{y}} = 0 = (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} )\cdot y + y  \end{equation}\]

Where \(r_1= (\mu_2+1)^2 + y^2, r_2= (x-\mu_1)^2 + y^2\) Equation for y-direction is rewritten as

\[\begin{equation} y \cdot [ 1- (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ) ] = 0 \end{equation}\]

So we have two kinds of the solution: > when \(y \neq 0\) is for the triangular points (\(L_4\) and \(L_5\)) > > when \(y = 0\) is for the collinear points (\(L_1, L_2\), and \(L_3\))

Triangular points (when \(y \neq 0\) )

From Equation

\[\begin{equation}  y \cdot [ 1- (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ) ] = 0 ~~~~(\because y \neq 0 ) \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \implies [ 1- (\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ) ] = 0  \end{equation} \]

Equation is multiplied by \((x+\mu_2)\)

\[\begin{equation} (x+\mu_2)- (x+\mu_2)(\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ) = 0 \\  (x+\mu_2)- (x+\mu_2)\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x+\mu_2)\frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}}  = 0 \end{equation}\]

From x-direction Equation

\[\begin{equation} x -[ (\mu_2+x) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + (x-\mu_1) \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ] = 0 \\ (\because \mu_1+\mu_2=1, ~~\mu_1 = 1- \mu_2) \\         x - (\mu_2+x) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x-1+\mu_2) \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}}  = 0  \\  x-(\mu_2+x) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x+\mu_2) \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} = 0  \end{equation}\]

By subtracting Equation from Equation

\[\begin{equation} \mu_2- \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} = 0 \\ \implies r_2 = 1 \end{equation}\]

Equation is multiplied by \((x-\mu_1)\)

\[\begin{equation}  (x-\mu_1)- (x-\mu_1)(\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ) = 0 \\          (x-\mu_1)- (x-\mu_1)\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x-\mu_1)\frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}}  = 0 \end{equation}\]

From x-direction Equation

\[\begin{equation} x -[ (\mu_2+x) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} + (x-\mu_1) \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} ] = 0 \\ (\because \mu_2 = 1- \mu_1) \\         x - (1- \mu_1+x) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x- \mu_1) \frac{ \mu_2}{r_2^{3}}  = 0  \\         x-(x-\mu_1) \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}}-\frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} - (x-\mu_1) \frac{\mu_2}{r_2^{3}} = 0 \end{equation}\]

By subtracting Equation from Equation

\[\begin{equation}  -\mu_1+ \frac{\mu_1}{r_1^{3}} = 0 \\ \implies r_1 = 1 \end{equation}\]

So \(r_1=r_2 = 1\), it is the distance between the two masses. Then, let's find the coordinate of \(L_4\) and \(L_5\)

From Equation

\[\begin{equation}  r_1^2 = (\mu_2+x)^2 + y^2 = 1 \\        \mu_2^2 + 2\mu_2 x + x^2 +y^2 = 1 \end{equation}\]

From Equation

\[\begin{equation} r_2^2 = (x-\mu_1)^2 + y^2 = 1 \\        x^2 - 2x\mu_1 + \mu_1^2 +y^2 = 1 \\        (\because \mu_1 = 1- \mu_2) \\       x^2 - 2x(1-\mu_2) + (1-\mu_2)^2 +y^2 = 1 \\        x^2 -2x + 2\mu_2x +1 - 2\mu_2 + \mu_2^2 + y^2 = 1 \end{equation}\]

By subtracting Equation from Equation

\[\begin{equation} 1-2\mu_2-2x =0 \\ \implies 2(\mu_2+x) = 1 \\ \implies x=\frac{1}{2}-\mu_2 \end{equation}\]

Substituting \(x = \frac{1}{2}-\mu_2\) into Equation

\[\begin{equation} (\mu_2+x)^2 + y^2 = 1 \\ \implies [\mu_2+ (\frac{1}{2}-\mu_2) ]^2+y^2 = 1 \\ \implies y^2 = 1- \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4} \\ \implies y = \frac{\pm \sqrt{3}}{2}  \end{equation}\]

Finally, we got the position of \(L_4\) and \(L_5\)

\(L_4\) Leading point: \(x=\frac{1}{2}-\mu_2\), \(y=\frac{+\sqrt{3}}{2}\)
\(L_5\) Trailing point: \(x=\frac{1}{2}-\mu_2\), \(y=\frac{- \sqrt{3}}{2}\)

Location of Lagrangian point \(L_4\) and \(L_5\)

Collinear points (when \(y = 0\) )

Equilibrium points lie along x-axis when \(y = 0\) , and satisfy \(\frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = 0\).

There are three solutions correspond to the "collinear Lagrangian equilibrium points" shown as Figure .

  • \(L_1\) point: between the mass \(\mu_1\) and \(\mu_2\)
  • \(L_2\) point: outside the mass \(\mu_2\) on the positive \(x\)-axis
  • \(L_3\) point: outside the mass \(\mu_1\) on the negative \(x\)-axis

Location of Lagrangian point \(L_1\), \(L_2\), and \(L_3\)

For the convenience of later calculation, we rewrite \(V(r)\), \(\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}\), and \(\frac{\partial V}{\partial y}\) as following: From Equation and Equation ,

Based on Equation , Equation becomes

\[\begin{equation} V(r) = \frac{1}{2}(x^2+y^2) + \frac{\mu_1}{r_1}+\frac{\mu_2}{r_2} \\ = \frac{1}{2}(\mu_1r_1^2 + \mu_2r_2^2 - \mu_1\mu_2) + \frac{\mu_1}{r_1}+\frac{\mu_2}{r_2} \\ = \frac{-1}{2}\mu_1\mu_2 +\mu_1\frac{r_1^2}{2}+\mu_2\frac{r_2^2}{2} + \frac{\mu_1}{r_1}+\frac{\mu_2}{r_2} \\ = \mu_1(\frac{1}{r_1}+\frac{r_1^2}{2}) + \mu_2(\frac{1}{r_2}+\frac{r_2^2}{2})-\frac{1}{2}\mu_1\mu_2 \\ \implies V(r) = \mu_1(\frac{1}{r_1}+\frac{r_1^2}{2}) + \mu_2(\frac{1}{r_2}+\frac{r_2^2}{2})-\frac{1}{2}\mu_1\mu_2 \end{equation}\]

According to Equation , \(\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}\) is rewritten as

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_1}\frac{\partial r_1}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_2}\frac{\partial r_2}{\partial x} = 0 \\ = \mu_1 \cdot (\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot[\frac{1}{2}((x+\mu_2)^2+y^2)^{1/2} \cdot 2(x+\mu_2)] \\ ~~~~ +\mu_2 \cdot (\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot [\frac{1}{2}((x-\mu_1)^2+y^2)^{1/2} \cdot 2(x-\mu_1)] \\ = \mu_1\cdot (\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \frac{(x+\mu_2)}{r_1} +\mu_2 \cdot(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2} \\ \implies \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot \frac{(x+\mu_2)}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2} \end{equation}\]

Also, \(\frac{\partial V}{\partial y}\) is rewritten as

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_1}\frac{\partial r_1}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial V}{\partial r_2}\frac{\partial r_2}{\partial y} = 0 \\ = \mu_1\cdot (\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot[\frac{1}{2}((x+\mu_2)^2+y^2)^{1/2} \cdot 2y] \\ ~~~~ +\mu_2 \cdot (\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot [\frac{1}{2}((x-\mu_1)^2+y^2)^{1/2} \cdot 2y] \\ = \mu_1 \cdot (\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \frac{y}{r_1} +\mu_2 \cdot (\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \frac{y}{r_2} \\ \implies \frac{\partial V}{\partial y} = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \frac{y}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \frac{y}{r_2} \end{equation}\]

Location of Lagrangian point \(L_1\)

For the \(L_1\) point, which is between the mass \(\mu_1\) and \(\mu_2\).

\[\begin{equation} r_1 + r_2 = 1, r_1 = x+\mu_2, r_2=-x+\mu_1 \end{equation}\]

Substituting the above condition into Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot \frac{(x+\mu_2)}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2} = 0 \\ = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot \frac{r_1}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot \frac{(-r_2)}{r_2} = 0 \\ ~~~~(\because r_1 = 1-r_2) \\ = \mu_1[\frac{-1}{(1-r_2)^2} + (1-r_2)] - \mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) = 0 \\ \implies \mu_1[\frac{-1}{(1-r_2)^2} + (1-r_2)] = \mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} = \frac{\frac{-1}{(1-r_2)^2} + (1-r_2)}{\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2} ~~~~(\because \times (1-r_1)^2 \cdot r_1^2) \\ = \frac{ -r_2^2 + (1-r_2)^3 r_2^2 }{-(1-r_2)^2 + (1-r_2)^2r_2^3 } \\ = \frac{ r_2^2 [(1-r_2)^3-1]}{ (1-r_2)^2(r_2^3 -1)} \\ = \frac{ r_2^2 (1-3r_2+3r_2^2-r_2^3-1)}{ (1-2r_2+r_2^2)(r_2^3 -1)} \\ = \frac{ 3r_2^3 (1-r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1-2r_2+r_2^2)(1-r_2^3)} \\ \end{equation}\]

Location of Lagrangian point \(L_2\)

\[\begin{equation} \implies \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} = \frac{\frac{-1}{(r_2+1)^2} + (r_2+1)}{\frac{1}{r_2^2}-r_2} ~~~~(\because \times (1+r_2)^2 \cdot r_2^2) \\ = \frac{ -r_2^2 + (r_2+1)^3 r_2^2 }{(r_2+1)^2 - (r_2+1)^2r_2^3 } \\ = \frac{ r_2^2 [(r_2+1)^3-1]}{ (r_2+1)^2(1-r_2^3 )} \\ = \frac{ r_2^2 (1+3r_2+3r_2^2+r_2^3-1)}{ (1-2r_2+r_2^2)(1-r_2^3)} \\ = \frac{ 3r_2^3 (1+r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1-2r_2+r_2^2)(1-r_2^3)} \\ = \frac{ 3r_2^3 (1+r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1+2r_2 +r_2^2 -r_2^3 -2r_2^4 -r_2^5) } \end{equation}\]

Let \(\alpha = (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{1/3}\)

\[\begin{equation} \alpha = (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ \frac{(1+r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1-2r_2+r_2^2)(1-r_2^3)} ]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [\frac{(1+r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1+2r_2 +r_2^2 -r_2^3 -2r_2^4 -r_2^5)} ]^{1/3} \end{equation}\]

Using Talyer's expression \(\frac{1}{1-x} = (1-x)^{-1} = 1+x+x^2+\mathcal{O}(x^3).\).. (if \(x \ll 1\))

The denominator of Equation becomes

\[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{1-x} = (1-x)^{-1} = 1+x+x^2+\mathcal{O}(x^3)\\ \implies \frac{1}{(1+2r_2 +r_2^2 -r_2^3 -2r_2^4 -r_2^5) } \\ \approx 1+x+x^2 \\ ( \because x = -2r_2-r_2^2 , \\ ~~~~ x^2 = (-2r_2-r_2^2)^2 = 4r_2^2+42r_2^3+r_2^4 ~~) \\ = 1 + 2r_2-r_2^2 + 4r_2^2+42r_2^3+r_2^4 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^7) \\ = 1 - 2r_2 + 3r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3) \\ \end{equation}\]

\(\mathcal{O}(r_2^3)\) is the omitted terms because the value is small to neglect.

Substituting the result of Equation into Equation

\[\begin{equation} \alpha = (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [\frac{(1+r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)}{ (1 - 2r_2 + 3r_2^2 ) } ]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ (1-r_2+\frac{1}{3}r_2^2)(1 + 2r_2 + 3r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3)) ]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ 1- 2r_2+ 3r_2^2 + r_2+ 2r_2^2+ 3r_2^3 + \frac{1}{3}r_2^2 - \frac{2}{3}r_2^3 + r_2^4]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ 1- r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2 + \frac{7}{3}r_2^3 + r_2^4 ]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ 1- r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3)]^{1/3} \end{equation}\]

Using binomial series \((1+x)^n = 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{3!}x^3 + \mathcal{O}(x^4)\)

The part of Equation \[\begin{equation} (1+x)^n = 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \mathcal{O}(x^3) \\ \implies = [1- r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3)]^{1/3} \\ \approx 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 \\ ( \because n= \frac{1}{3} ,~~x = -r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2 ,~~ \\ x^2 = (-r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2)^2 = r_2^2 - \frac{8}{3}r_2^3+\frac{16}{9}r_2^4 ~~~)\\ = 1+ \frac{1}{3}(- r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2) + \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{3})(\frac{1}{3}-1)(r_2^2 -\frac{8}{3}r_2^3+\frac{16}{9}r_2^4) \\ = 1- \frac{1}{3}r_2 + \frac{4}{9}r_2^2 + \frac{-1}{9}(r_2^2- \frac{8}{3}r_2^3+\frac{16}{9}r_2^4) \\ = 1- \frac{1}{3}r_2 + \frac{1}{3}r_2^2 + \frac{8}{27}r_2^3 -\frac{16}{81}r_2^4 \\ = 1- \frac{1}{3}r_2 + \frac{1}{3}r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3) \end{equation}\]

Therefore, Substituting the result of Equation on Equation

\[\begin{equation} \alpha = (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [ 1- r_2 + \frac{4}{3}r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3)]^{1/3} \\ = r_2 [1- \frac{1}{3}r_2 + \frac{1}{3}r_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^3)] \\ = r_2 - \frac{1}{3} r_2^2 + \frac{1}{3}r_2^3 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^4) \\ \end{equation}\]

Here, using Lagrangian's inversion method as same as \(L_1\) to invert the series expansions in Equation .

\[\begin{equation} r_2 = \alpha + \frac{1}{3}[r_2^2-r_2^3 +\mathcal{O}(r_2^4) ] \\ = \alpha + (\frac{1}{3})\phi(r_2) ~~~~(\because e= \frac{1}{3}) \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \implies \phi(r_2) = r_2^2 - r_2^3 + \mathcal{O}(r_2^4) \end{equation}\]

And hence

\[\begin{equation} \phi(\alpha) = \alpha^2 - \alpha^3  \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} [\phi(\alpha)]^2 = \alpha^4 - 2\alpha^5 + \alpha^6  \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \frac{d}{d\alpha}[\phi(\alpha)]^2  = 4\alpha^3 - 10\alpha^4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^5) \end{equation}\]

Substituting the result of Equation , , and into Equation

\[\begin{equation} r_2 = \alpha + \sum_{j=1}^{+\infty} \frac{(\frac{1}{3})^j}{j!} \frac{d^{j-1}}{dz^{j-1}}[\phi{z}]^j \\ = \alpha + \frac{\frac{1}{3}}{1!}\phi(\alpha) + \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{9})\frac{d}{d\alpha}[\phi(\alpha)]^2 + ...\\ = \alpha + \frac{1}{3}(\alpha^2-\alpha^3) + \frac{1}{18}(4\alpha^3 - 10\alpha^4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^5) ) \\ = \alpha + \frac{1}{3}\alpha^2 -\frac{1}{3}\alpha^3 +\frac{2}{9}\alpha^3 - \frac{5}{9}\alpha^4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^5) \\ = \alpha + \frac{1}{3}\alpha^2 -\frac{1}{9}\alpha^3 - \frac{5}{9}\alpha^4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^5) \\ \implies r_2 \approx \alpha(1+\frac{1}{3}\alpha-\frac{1}{9}\alpha^2 -\frac{5}{9}\alpha^3) \end{equation}\]

From previous condition,

\[\begin{equation} r_2 = x - \mu_1 \\ \implies x = \mu_1 + r_2 \\ = \mu_1 + \alpha + \frac{1}{3}\alpha^2 - \frac{1}{9}\alpha^3 - \frac{5}{9}\alpha^4 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^5) \\ = \mu_1 + (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{1/3} + \frac{1}{3} (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{2/3} - \frac{1}{9} (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{3/3} - \frac{5}{9} (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{4/3} + \mathcal{O}( (\frac{\mu_2}{3\mu_1})^{5/3} \end{equation}\]

This is location of \(L_2\) point on x-axis.

Location of Lagrangian point \(L_3\)

For the \(L_3\) point, which is outside the mass \(\mu_1\) on the negative x-axis.

\[\begin{equation} r_2 - r_1 = 1, r_1 = -x-\mu_2, r_2=-x+\mu_1 \end{equation}\]

Substituting the above condition into Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\partial V}{\partial x} = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot \frac{(x+\mu_2)}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{-1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot \frac{(x-\mu_1)}{r_2} = 0 \\ = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot \frac{(-r_1)}{r_1} +\mu_2(\frac{1}{r_2^2}+r_2) \cdot \frac{r_2}{r_2} = 0 ~~~~~(\because r_2 = 1+ r_1) \\ = \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) \cdot (-1) +\mu_2[\frac{1}{(1+ r_1)^2}+(1+ r_1)] = 0 \\ \implies \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) = \mu_2[\frac{1}{(1+ r_1)^2}+(1+ r_1)] \end{equation}\]

Here, let \(r_1 = 1+ \beta\), \(r_2 = 1+r_1 = 2+\beta\)

\[\begin{equation} \mu_1(\frac{-1}{r_1^2}+r_1) = \mu_2[\frac{1}{(1+ r_1)^2}+(1+ r_1)] \\ \implies \mu_1(\frac{-1}{(1+\beta)^2}+ (1+\beta)) = \mu_2[\frac{1}{(1+ (1+\beta))^2}+(1+ (1+\beta))] \\ \implies \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} = [\frac{-1}{(1+\beta)^2}+ (1+\beta)] \cdot [\frac{1}{(2+\beta)^2}+(2+\beta))]^{-1} \end{equation}\]

Using binomial series, calculate the part of Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{(1+\beta)^2} = (1+\beta)^{-2} \\ = (1+x)^n \\ = 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2 + \mathcal{O}(x^3) \\ ~~~(\because n=-2, ~ x=\beta) \\ = 1 - 2\beta + \frac{(-2)(-3)}{2}\beta^2 \\ = 1 - 2\beta + 3\beta^3 \end{equation}\]

Using binomial series, calculate the part of Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{1}{(2+\beta)^2} = \frac{1}{(4+4\beta+4\beta^2)} \\ = \frac{1}{4(1+\beta+\frac{1}{4}\beta^2)} \\ = \frac{1}{4}( 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2) \\ (\because n=-1~, ~ x=\beta+ \frac{1}{4}\beta^2, \\ x^2 =(\beta+ \frac{1}{4}\beta^2)^2 = \beta^2 + \frac{1}{2}\beta^3 +\frac{1}{16}\beta^4 ~~) \\ = \frac{1}{4} (1-\beta-\frac{1}{4}\beta^2 + \beta^2 +\frac{1}{2}\beta^3 + \frac{1}{16}\beta^4 ) \\ = \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\beta + \frac{3}{16}\beta^2 +\frac{1}{8}\beta^3 +\frac{1}{64}\beta^4 \\ = \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\beta + \frac{3}{16}\beta^2 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^3) \end{equation}\]

Substituting the result of Equation and into Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} = [\frac{-1}{(1+\beta)^2}+ (1+\beta)] \cdot [\frac{1}{(2+\beta)^2}+(2+\beta))]^{-1} \\ = ( -1+ 2\beta - 3\beta^3 +1+ \beta) \cdot (\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{4}\beta + \frac{3}{16}\beta^2 -2-\beta)^{-1} \\ = (3\beta -3\beta^2)(\frac{-7}{4}-\frac{5}{4}\beta+\frac{3}{16}\beta^2 )^{-1} \end{equation}\]

The part of Equation

\[\begin{equation} (\frac{-7}{4}-\frac{5}{4}\beta+\frac{3}{16}\beta^2 )^{-1} = \frac{1}{(\frac{-7}{4}-\frac{5}{4}\beta+\frac{3}{16}\beta^2 )} \\ = \frac{1}{\frac{-7}{4}(1+\frac{5}{7}\beta-\frac{3}{28}\beta^2)} \\ = \frac{-4}{7}\frac{1}{(1+\frac{5}{7}\beta-\frac{3}{28}\beta^2)} \\ = \frac{-4}{7}( 1+nx + \frac{n(n-1)}{2!}x^2) \\ (\because n= -1~, ~ x=\frac{5}{7}\beta-\frac{3}{28}\beta^2, \\ ~~~ x^2 =(\frac{5}{7}\beta-\frac{3}{28}\beta^2)^2 = \frac{35}{49}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 +\frac{9}{784}\beta^4 ~~) \\ = \frac{-4}{7}[1-\frac{5}{7}\beta+\frac{3}{28}\beta^2 + \frac{1}{2}(-1)(-2)(\frac{35}{49}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 +\frac{9}{784}\beta^4) ] \\ = \frac{-4}{7}[1-\frac{5}{7}\beta+\frac{3}{28}\beta^2 + \frac{35}{49}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 +\frac{9}{784}\beta^4 ] \\ = \frac{-4}{7}[1-\frac{5}{7}\beta+ \frac{3}{28}\beta^2 + \frac{35}{49}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 + \frac{9}{784}\beta^4 ] \\ = \frac{-4}{7}[1-\frac{5}{7}\beta+ \frac{161}{1372}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) ] \end{equation}\]

Substituting the result of Equation into Equation

\[\begin{equation} \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} = (3\beta -3\beta^2)(\frac{-7}{4}-\frac{5}{4}\beta+\frac{3}{16}\beta^2 )^{-1} \\ = 3(\beta -\beta^2) \cdot \frac{-4}{7}[1-\frac{5}{7}\beta+ \frac{161}{1372}\beta^2 - \frac{30}{196}\beta^3 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) \\ = \frac{-12}{7}[\beta - \frac{5}{7}\beta^2 + \frac{161}{1372}\beta^3 - \beta + \frac{5}{7}\beta^3- \frac{161}{1372}\beta^4 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5)] \\ = \frac{-12}{7}[\beta - \frac{12}{7}\beta^2 + \frac{163}{196}\beta^3 -\frac{161}{1372}\beta^4 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) ] \\ = \frac{-12}{7}\beta + \frac{144}{49}\beta^2 - \frac{1956}{1372}\beta^3 + \frac{1932}{9604}\beta^4 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) \\ \implies \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} =\frac{-12}{7}\beta + \frac{144}{49}\beta^2-\frac{1956}{1372}\beta^3 + \frac{1932}{9604}\beta^4 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) \end{equation}\]

Let \(\alpha = \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1}\), and \(k= \frac{12}{7}\beta\) , the higher terms of Equation is neglected, because the value id small.

\[\begin{equation} \alpha = \frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1} =\frac{-12}{7}\beta + \frac{144}{49}\beta^2 -\frac{1956}{1372}\beta^3 + \frac{1932}{9604}\beta^4 + \mathcal{O}(\beta^5) \\ = \frac{-12}{7}\beta + \frac{144}{49}\beta^2 +\mathcal{O}(\beta^3) \\ = -k + k^2 +\mathcal{O}(k^3) \end{equation}\]

Using Lagrangian’s inversion method to invert the series expansions in Equation

\[\begin{equation} k = -\alpha + k^2 +\mathcal{O}(k^3) \\ = -\alpha + (1)\cdot \phi(k) ~~~~(\because e = 1 ) \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \implies \phi(k) = k^2 + \mathcal{O}(k^3) \end{equation}\]

And hence

\[\begin{equation} \implies \phi(\alpha) = \alpha^2 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^3) \end{equation}\]

\[\begin{equation} \frac{12}{7}\beta = k = -\alpha + \alpha^2 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^3) \\ \implies \beta = \frac{-7}{12}\alpha + \frac{7}{12}\alpha^2 + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^3) \\ = \frac{-7}{12} (\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1}) + \frac{7}{12}((\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1})^2) + \mathcal{O}((\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1})^3) \end{equation}\]

From previous condition,

\[\begin{equation} r_1 = 1 + \beta \\ = x + \mu_2 \\ \implies x = -r_1 - \mu_2 \\ = -(1+\beta) - \mu_2 \\ = -1 +(\frac{-7}{12}\alpha + \frac{7}{12}\alpha^2) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha^3) - \mu_2 \\ = -1 - \mu_2 + \frac{-7}{12} (\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1}) - \frac{7}{12}((\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1})^2) + \mathcal{O}((\frac{\mu_2}{\mu_1})^3) \end{equation}\]

This is location of \(L_3\) point on the negative \(x\)-axis.
